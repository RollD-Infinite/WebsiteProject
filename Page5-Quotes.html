Why I Chose These Quotes

I collect quotes and snappy sayings. Usually, I make them into cross stitch patterns. There is something about using a traditional craft to make something modern. Especially where there is a disconnect between the style, such as a “granny sampler” with all the cutesy little flowers and borders, and the saying. The trend is called “subversive cross stitch” and has been a big thing for the last decade or so. I’ve designed my own patterns and I’ve stitched patterns designed by other people. They’re all very satisfying to work.

Even though I like the jarring feel of subversive stitching, I think the quote collection is more about having something unusual to say that makes me, or others, stop and think. Even the most throw-away, mindless quote has something to say. An observation about modern culture or a deep spiritual meaning or even just an excuse to laugh and say “that’s so true!”

Many of the quotes on the list here are from media I’ve been watching/reading/listening to lately. Or they’re just classics. If you are looking for one or two to dig deeper into, I’d suggest reading ‘The Silver Chair’ (1953) by C.S. Lewis or watching ‘Avatar: The Last Airbender’ (2005).

Puddleglum is the most cheerful downer, the most optimistic pessimist, and the deepest thinker in the book. I avoided his most famous quote in this list, partly because I had enough philosophical quotes, but mostly because the “Respectowiggle” scene is my daughter’s favorite. Iroh is the wise elder trope in ‘Avatar’. He is goofy at times, but if you watch him, even in his humor there is wisdom to be found. The sequel series, ‘The Legend of Korra’ shows even more of Iroh’s wit and wisdom.

Hopefully, something in this list will strike you enough to look deeper into the author. Or make everyone around you wonder why you’re laughing too hard. Either way I win.
